Khulna


Khulna is Bangladesh's third-largest city, after Dhaka and Chittagong. In the south-western part of the country, on the Rupsha and Bhairab Rivers, it covers an area of 59.57 square kilometres (23.00 sq mi); the district covers 4,394.46 square kilometres (1,696.71 sq mi). On the bank of Rupsha (and Bhairav) river, it is known as the industrial city and located in southern part of Bangladesh. A large part of the Sundarbans, a UNESCO world heritage is located in Khulna. It has many Jute mills, the only newsprint mill and hardboard mill of Bangladesh, the only state-owned match factory (Dada match, later Dhaka match), Oxygen company (BOL), Ship yard. Khulna is the 3rd largest city in Bangladesh after Dhaka and Chittagong. It is a fast developing, provided with many park, resorts and a peaceful city inhabited by almost 2 million people.


Location:
Khulna is south of Jessore and Narail, east of Satkhira, west of Bagerhat and north of the Bay of Bengal. Distance from Chittagonng to Khulna is 349 Km and the distance from Dhaka is 273 Km.

Best season for visiting : Early November to early March is the best season to visit Khulna.

Get in :
From Chittagong-
  • By road: Both AC and Non-AC buses are available on this road. Ticket price for the Non-AC Bus services are 1050-1100 Tk. and AC bus services are 1400 Tk. Operators: Hanif Enterprise, Soudia Coach Service.
  • By Air: Air ticket prices can vary around 7000-9000taka. Available airlines are Biman Bangladesh Airlines, US-Bangla Airlines and NovoAir.
  • By Train:There is no direct train from Chittagong.At first you have to go Dhaka and then you have to get in another train.
