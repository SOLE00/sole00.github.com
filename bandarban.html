Bandarban

Bandarban, a part of the Chittagong Division is a district in south-eastern Bangladesh. It is a part of the three Chittagong Hill Tracts; the other two being Rangamati District and Khagrachhari District. It is the remotest and least populated district in Bangladesh. The lure of the tallest peaks of Bangladesh, treks through virgin forests and chance to meet more than 15 tribes of the region up close is growing both among Bangladeshis and tourists from other countries. A nearly 52 km² hill-town housing about 32,000 people, of which the majority are Marma. There is a Tribal Cultural Institute, which features a library and a museum. Along the numerous kyangs and mosques, there is a temple dedicated to Kali, the most revered goddess of Hindus in Bangladesh, as well as a centre maintained by ISKON. The three highest peaks of Bangladesh – Tahjindong (1280 meters, also known as Bijoy), Mowdok Mual (1052 meters), and Keokradong (883 metres) – are located in Bandarban district, as well as Raikhiang Lake, the highest lake in Bangladesh.

Location:
Bandarban is located in between 21°11' and 22°22' north latitudes and in between 92°04' and 92°41' east longitudes. It is bounded by Rangamati district on the north, Arakan (Myanmar) on the south, Chin Province (Myanmar) and Rangamati district on the east, Chittagong and Cox's bazar district on the west.

Main points to visit in Bandarban :
Nafakhum, Amiakhum, Boga Lake, Golden Temple, Meghla, Nilgiri, Nilachal, Chimbuk hill.

Best season for visiting :Winter
Get in:
From Chittagong- The way to go to Bandarban from Chittagong is by bus, Private car or Motorbike. The easiest way is by bus. From Shah Amanat Bridge or From Bohoddarhat Bus Terminal one can choose S.Alam Bus service or Marsa Bus Service. The costing for per seat will be 80 taka which is for Non AC bus. One can also take taxi service from Uber which will cost more then 1000 taka according to exact destination. Personal cars and bikes can also be used as the transport.
