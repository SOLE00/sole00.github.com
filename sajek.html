Sajek

Sajek Tripuri Valley is one of the most popular tourist spots in Bangladesh situated among the hills of the Kasalong range of mountains in Sajek union, Baghaichhari Upazila in Rangamati District. The valley is 2,000 feet (610 m) above sea level.[4] Sajek Tripuri Valley is known as the Queen of Hills & Roof of Rangamati. The name of Sajek Valley came from the Sajek River that originates from Karnafuli river. The Sajek river works as a border between Bangladesh and India.


Location:
Sajek is a union located in the north of Chittagong Hill Tracts. It's under Baghaichori Upazila in Rangamati hill district, it is situated 67 kilometres (42 mi) north-east from Khagrachhari town and 95 kilometres (59 mi) north from Rangamati city. The border of Bangladesh and Mizoram of India is 8 kilometres (5.0 mi) east from Sajek

Main points to visit in Sajek :
Sajek valley is known for its natural environment and is surrounded by mountains, dense forest, and grassland hill tracks. Many small rivers flow through the mountains among which the Kachalong and the Machalong are notable. One can also visit the army camp.

Best season for visiting : Winter and Summer. Since the roads are very narrow, so going in rainy season is full of risk.
Directions :
From Chittagong-
• Chittagong to Khagrachhori busway is available and it needs maximum of four hours.
• From Khagrachhori to Dighinala there are local buses, fare per head Tk 45/-.
• From Dighinala, three types of vehicles are available. Four wheels jeep, locally named as Chander Gari, accommodates 12-15 persons– rental return fare(8,500-10,000/- BDT). CNG accommodates 5 persons rental return fare 2500/- to 3000 /-, as it is risky, so you should consider whether to hire it. Another transport system is the motorcycle, it accommodates 3 persons including the driver, return fare 1000/- to 1200/-.
Accommodation and Costs : Sajek Resort, Sajek Classic Resort, Meghadree Resort, Megh Kabbo Eco resort, Nil Pahari Eco resort. The costing will vary according to hotels.
