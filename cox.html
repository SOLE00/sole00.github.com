Cox's Bazar

Cox's Bazar is a city, fishing port, tourism centre, and district headquarters in southeastern Bangladesh. It is famous mostly for being the longest sea beach in the world. The modern Cox's Bazar derives its name from Captain Hiram Cox, an officer of the British East India Company, a Superintendent of Palongkee outpost. To commemorate his role in refugee rehabilitation work, a market was established and named after him. The city covers an area of 23.4 km2 (9.0 sq mi) with 27 mahallas and 9 wards and as of 2011 had a population of 265,500. Cox's Bazar is connected by road and air with Chittagong.


Location:
It is located 150 km (93 mi) south of the city of Chittagong.

Main points to visit in Cox's Bazar:
Marine Drive Road, Coxs Bazar Beach, Innani Beach, Himchori Beach, Teknaf, Radiant Fish World.

Best season for visiting : Any season is favourable to visit Coxs Bazar. But Winter is the best.
Directions :
From Chittagong-
Chittagong to Cox's Bazar busway is available and it needs maximum of four hours. From Shah Amanat Bridge to Coxs Bazar Dolphin Square there are local buses, fare per head Tk 170/-. Coach services like Marsa, Green Line, S.Alam are also available, with options for AC or non-AC bus.

Accommodation and Costs : Some renowned resorts include: Ocean Paradise, Long Beach, Royal Tulip, Sayeman Beach Resort, Neeshorgo Beach Resort etc. The costing will vary according to hotels. Starting from 4500-30000 taka, rooms can be the rent for 2/3/4 persons per night.

Suggested Resturants and Foods: Kutumbari restaurant, Kashbon restaurant, KFC restaurant, POUSHEE, HANDI, MERMAID.
Famous foods to try- FISH BBQ and also famous for BHORTA items.
